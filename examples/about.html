The Algeria Research Hub is a project funded by the US Goverment, the Department of State, and the US Embassy in Algiers. This project started in October 2021 to improve the research capacity and collaboration between Algerian researchers and North American researchers especially in Artificial Intellegence and Applied Math. All research efforts have the emphasists to develop research iniatives that hopes to benefit the Algerian economy and/or the most vulnerable populations within Algeria. In it's first engagement phase, the program will mostly work with researchers from Sidi Abdullah with the hopes of integrating more research institutions as the project progresses. The Pulte Institute for Global Development at the University of Notre Dame is the implementing partner on the project. The purpose of this site is to enable information sharing, networking, and research best practices.
